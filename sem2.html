2 Mark Questions and Answers

Question 1: What do you mean by an acceptor & a rejector circuit?

Acceptor Circuit (Series LCR Circuit): A series LCR circuit is referred to as an "acceptor circuit". Its impedance is given by \( Z = \sqrt{R^2 + (X_L - X_C)^2} \). At the resonant frequency (\(f_0\)), the inductive reactance (\(X_L\)) equals the capacitive reactance (\(X_C\)), making the total impedance minimum and purely resistive (\(Z = R\)). This minimum impedance allows the maximum possible current to flow. Therefore, the circuit "accepts" signals at its resonant frequency.

Rejector Circuit (Parallel LCR Circuit): A parallel LCR circuit is known as a "rejector circuit". At its resonant frequency, it offers an extremely high impedance to the external circuit. This high impedance severely restricts or "rejects" the flow of current from the source at that specific frequency, acting as a band-stop filter.

Question 2: State Thevenin's theorem.

Statement: Thevenin's theorem states that any linear, two-terminal electrical network can be replaced by an equivalent circuit consisting of a single independent voltage source (\(V_{th}\)) in series with a single resistor (\(R_{th}\)).

  • Thevenin Voltage (\(V_{th}\)): The open-circuit voltage across the two terminals.
  • Thevenin Resistance (\(R_{th}\)): The equivalent resistance seen from the terminals after all independent sources are turned off (voltage sources shorted, current sources opened).

Question 3: The magnetic susceptibility of Bismuth is \(-0.12 \times 10^{-5}\). Calculate relative permeability.

The formula is: \( \mu_r = 1 + \chi_m \).

Given: \( \chi_m = -0.12 \times 10^{-5} = -0.0000012 \).

Calculation: \( \mu_r = 1 + (-0.0000012) = 0.9999988 \).

The relative permeability of Bismuth is 0.9999988, confirming it is diamagnetic.

Question 4: Find the energy required to build up a current 'I' in a circuit of self-inductance 'L'.

The energy is stored in the magnetic field and is equal to the work done against the back EMF (\(\epsilon = -L \frac{dI}{dt}\)). The total work \(U\) is found by integrating the power: \( U = \int_0^I L i \, dI \).

The formula for the stored energy is: $$ U = \frac{1}{2} LI^2 $$

Question 5: Write the relation between electrical susceptibility & dielectric constant.

The relationship is derived from the electric displacement vector \( \vec{D} = \epsilon_0\vec{E} + \vec{P} \). The final relation is:

$$ K = 1 + \chi_e \quad \text{or} \quad \epsilon_r = 1 + \chi_e $$

Where \(K\) (or \(\epsilon_r\)) is the dielectric constant and \(\chi_e\) is the electrical susceptibility.

Question 6: Define logarithmic decrement of a Ballistic galvanometer.

The logarithmic decrement (\(\lambda\)) of a damped oscillating system is the natural logarithm of the ratio of the amplitudes of any two successive oscillations on the same side of the equilibrium position.

If \(\theta_1\) and \(\theta_{n+1}\) are the first and n-th amplitudes: $$ \lambda = \frac{1}{n} \ln\left(\frac{\theta_1}{\theta_{n+1}}\right) $$

Question 7: The electric field on the surface of a thin spherical shell of radius 0.5 m is 800 N/C. Find the net flux.

The net electric flux is \( \Phi = E \times A \).

Surface area of the sphere: \( A = 4\pi r^2 = 4\pi(0.5)^2 = \pi \) m².

Net flux: \( \Phi = 800 \times \pi \approx 2513.3 \) N·m²/C.

Question 8: Write Laplace's & Poisson's equation.

Poisson's Equation: Relates potential \(V\) to charge density \(\rho\): $$ \nabla^2V = -\frac{\rho}{\epsilon_0} $$

Laplace's Equation: Applies to charge-free regions (\(\rho = 0\)): $$ \nabla^2V = 0 $$

Question 9: Explain the conservative nature of the electrostatic field.

An electrostatic field is conservative because the work done moving a charge between two points is path-independent. This means the line integral of the electric field around any closed path is zero: $$ \oint \vec{E} \cdot d\vec{l} = 0 $$ This is because the field can be expressed as the negative gradient of a scalar potential, \( \vec{E} = -\nabla V \).

Question 10: Find the relation between electric field intensity & potential gradient.

The electric field intensity (\(\vec{E}\)) is the negative of the potential gradient (\(\nabla V\)): $$ \vec{E} = -\nabla V $$ The negative sign indicates the field points in the direction of the steepest decrease in potential.

Question 11: Explain electrical image.

The method of electrical images is a technique that replaces complex boundary conditions (like conductors) with a simpler system of fictitious "image charges". These image charges are placed outside the region of interest to reproduce the correct potential on the conductor surfaces, simplifying the field calculation.

Question 12: State Lorentz force law.

The Lorentz force law describes the total force (\(\vec{F}\)) on a charge \(q\) moving with velocity \(\vec{v}\) in an electric field \(\vec{E}\) and a magnetic field \(\vec{B}\):

$$ \vec{F} = q\vec{E} + q(\vec{v} \times \vec{B}) $$

Question 13: The magnetic field at a point near the center but outside of a current-carrying solenoid is zero. Explain why.

For an ideal, infinitely long solenoid, the magnetic field is perfectly confined inside. For a real solenoid, the external field lines spread out over a vast area, making the magnetic field strength just outside the central part extremely weak and negligible.

Question 14: The ratio of the Electric field vector (E) & Magnetic field vector (H) has the dimension of which physical quantity?

The ratio \(E/H\) has the dimensions of electrical impedance or resistance. Units: \( \frac{V/m}{A/m} = \frac{V}{A} = \Omega \) (Ohms). This is known as the wave impedance of the medium.

Question 15: Explain polarization of a dielectric material.

Polarization is the process where a dielectric material develops a net dipole moment when placed in an external electric field. This alignment of dipoles creates an internal electric field that opposes the external field, reducing the net field inside the dielectric.

Question 16: Find the relation between relative permeability & magnetic susceptibility.

The relationship is given by the linear equation: $$ \mu_r = 1 + \chi_m $$

Question 17: Explain how Lenz's law obeys the principle of conservation of energy.

Lenz's law states that an induced current opposes the change in flux that caused it. This opposition requires external mechanical work to be done. This work is converted into the electrical energy of the induced current, thus perfectly conserving energy.

Question 18: State Kirchhoff's laws for an AC circuit.

Using phasors (complex numbers for magnitude and phase):

  • Kirchhoff's Current Law (KCL): The phasor sum of all currents entering a junction is zero. \( \sum \tilde{I} = 0 \).
  • Kirchhoff's Voltage Law (KVL): The phasor sum of all potential differences around a closed loop is zero. \( \sum \tilde{V} = 0 \).

Question 19: In a series LCR circuit, \(L = 20\) mH, \(C = 60\) mF & \(R = 40 \, \Omega\). Find resonant frequency.

The resonant frequency (\(f_0\)) is given by \( f_0 = \frac{1}{2\pi\sqrt{LC}} \).

Calculation: \( f_0 = \frac{1}{2\pi\sqrt{(0.02)(0.06)}} \approx 4.60 \) Hz.

Question 20: Electric field is \( \vec{E} = 5\hat{i} + 4\hat{j} + 3\hat{k} \). Calculate flux through a surface area of 100 units in the XY plane.

Flux \( \Phi = \vec{E} \cdot \vec{A} \). The area vector for a surface in the XY-plane is \( \vec{A} = 100\hat{k} \).

Calculation: \( \Phi = (5\hat{i} + 4\hat{j} + 3\hat{k}) \cdot (100\hat{k}) = 300 \) units.

Question 21: What is an Electric dipole? Write the expression for the Moment of an electric dipole.

Electric Dipole: A system of two equal and opposite point charges (\(+q\) and \(-q\)) separated by a small vector distance \(\vec{d}\).

Electric Dipole Moment (\(\vec{p}\)): A vector measuring the dipole's strength, directed from \(-q\) to \(+q\). Expression: $$ \vec{p} = q\vec{d} $$

Question 22: What do you mean by dielectric polarization & State & explain Ampere's circuital law?

Dielectric Polarization: The process where a dielectric material develops a net dipole moment when placed in an external electric field.

Ampere's Circuital Law: States that the line integral of the magnetic field \(\vec{B}\) around any closed path is equal to \(\mu_0\) times the total steady current \(I_{enc}\) enclosed by the path. $$ \oint \vec{B} \cdot d\vec{l} = \mu_0 I_{enc} $$

Question 23: Give the vector form of Faraday's law.

Integral Form: The EMF around a closed loop equals the negative rate of change of magnetic flux through the surface. $$ \oint \vec{E} \cdot d\vec{l} = -\frac{d\Phi_B}{dt} $$

Differential Form: The curl of the electric field equals the negative time derivative of the magnetic field. $$ \nabla \times \vec{E} = -\frac{\partial \vec{B}}{\partial t} $$

Question 24: Define self-inductance, give its unit & dimension.

  • Definition: The property of a circuit to oppose a change in current, defined as \( L = N\Phi / I \).
  • Unit: The Henry (H).
  • Dimension: [M L² T⁻² A⁻²].

Question 25: A current of 5A produces a flux of \(2 \times 10^{-3}\) Wb through a coil of 500 turns. Calculate the energy stored in the magnetic field.

1. Calculate Inductance (L): \( L = \frac{N\Phi}{I} = \frac{500 \times 2 \times 10^{-3}}{5} = 0.2 \) H.

2. Calculate Stored Energy (U): \( U = \frac{1}{2}LI^2 = \frac{1}{2}(0.2)(5^2) = 2.5 \) Joules.

Question 26: In a series LCR circuit, the resonant frequency is 800 Hz. The half power points are obtained at frequencies 745 Hz & 855 Hz. Calculate the value of Q.

The Quality Factor \( Q = f_r / \Delta f \).

Bandwidth, \( \Delta f = f_2 - f_1 = 855 - 745 = 110 \) Hz.

Calculation: \( Q = \frac{800}{110} \approx 7.27 \).

Question 27: Why is a parallel resonance circuit called a rejector circuit?

A parallel resonance circuit is called a rejector circuit because at its resonant frequency, its total impedance becomes maximum. This maximum impedance blocks or "rejects" current at that frequency from being drawn from the source.

Question 28: State Reciprocity theorem.

The reciprocity theorem states that in a linear network, if a voltage source V in branch 'A' produces a current I in branch 'B', then moving the voltage source V to branch 'B' will produce the same current I in branch 'A'.

Question 29: Potential is given by \(V = x(3y^2 - x^2 + z)\). Find the components of the electric field.

Using \( \vec{E} = -\nabla V \), where \( V = 3xy^2 - x^3 + xz \):

  • \( E_x = -\frac{\partial V}{\partial x} = -(3y^2 - 3x^2 + z) = 3x^2 - 3y^2 - z \)
  • \( E_y = -\frac{\partial V}{\partial y} = -(6xy) = -6xy \)
  • \( E_z = -\frac{\partial V}{\partial z} = -(x) = -x \)

Question 30: Obtain the differential form of Gauss's law.

Start with the integral form, \( \oint \vec{E} \cdot d\vec{A} = Q_{enc} / \epsilon_0 \). Using the Divergence Theorem and expressing charge as a volume integral, we equate the integrands to get the differential form: $$ \nabla \cdot \vec{E} = \frac{\rho}{\epsilon_0} $$

Question 31: A charge distribution is \( \rho(r) = \rho_0(1 - r^2/R^2) \) for \( r \le R \). Calculate total charge.

The total charge \(Q\) is \( \int \rho dV = \int_0^R \rho_0(1 - \frac{r^2}{R^2}) (4\pi r^2) dr \).

Integrating gives: \( Q = 4\pi\rho_0 [\frac{r^3}{3} - \frac{r^5}{5R^2}]_0^R = 4\pi\rho_0 (\frac{R^3}{3} - \frac{R^3}{5}) \).

Final Answer: $$ Q = \frac{8}{15}\pi\rho_0R^3 $$

Question 32: Define electric susceptibility, dielectric constant, & obtain a relation between them.

Susceptibility (\(\chi_e\)): Relates polarization to E-field: \( \vec{P} = \epsilon_0\chi_e\vec{E} \).

Dielectric Constant (\(K\)): The factor by which the E-field is reduced in a material.

Relation: $$ K = 1 + \chi_e $$

Question 33: Obtain the Self-inductance of a solenoid.

For a solenoid with N turns, length L, and area A: The magnetic field is \( B = \mu_0 n I \). The total flux is \( \Phi_{total} = N(BA) = \frac{\mu_0 N^2 I A}{L} \). Since \(L_{ind} = \Phi_{total}/I\), we get:

$$ L = \frac{\mu_0 N^2 A}{L} $$

Question 34: State & explain Kirchhoff's laws.

  • KCL (Current Law): The sum of currents entering a junction is zero (conservation of charge).
  • KVL (Voltage Law): The sum of potential differences around a closed loop is zero (conservation of energy).

Question 35: What is the use of complex numbers in AC circuits?

Complex numbers simplify AC analysis by representing sinusoidal signals as single "phasors". This turns differential equations into simpler algebraic equations like \( \tilde{V} = \tilde{I}Z \).

Question 36: Plot the variation of impedance, reactance, and current for frequency in a series LCR circuit.

  • Reactance: Inductive reactance \(X_L\) is a line from the origin. Capacitive \(X_C\) is a curve from infinity.
  • Impedance (Z): A "U"-shaped curve, minimum (\(Z=R\)) at resonance.
  • Current (I): An inverted "U" curve, maximum at resonance.

Question 37: Give the steps to convert a current source to a voltage source.

For a current source \(I_s\) in parallel with \(R_p\):

  1. Calculate Voltage: \(V_{eq} = I_s \times R_p\).
  2. Determine Resistance: \(R_{eq} = R_p\).
  3. The new circuit is \(V_{eq}\) in series with \(R_{eq}\).

Question 38: State & explain Norton's theorem.

Norton's theorem states any linear network can be replaced by a current source (\(I_N\)) in parallel with a resistor (\(R_N\)). \(I_N\) is the short-circuit current, and \(R_N\) is the equivalent resistance with sources turned off.

Question 39: Write Poisson's equation for a distribution of charge of given potential function.

Poisson's equation allows finding the charge distribution \(\rho\) from a known potential function \(V\): $$ \nabla^2V = -\frac{\rho}{\epsilon_0} $$ By calculating the Laplacian of \(V\), one can solve for \(\rho\).

Question 40: State the uniqueness theorem.

The Uniqueness Theorem states that for a region with specified boundary conditions, the solution to Poisson's/Laplace's equation is unique. If we find one solution that fits, it is the only solution.

Question 41: A solenoidal coil has 100 turns, length 50 cm & carrying current 1 mA. Calculate B inside.

Turns per length \(n = 100 / 0.5 = 200\) m⁻¹.

\(B = \mu_0 n I = (4\pi \times 10^{-7})(200)(10^{-3}) \approx 2.51 \times 10^{-7}\) T or 0.251 \(\mu\)T.

Question 42: Define electrical susceptibility in terms of the polarization vector.

Electrical susceptibility \(\chi_e\) is defined by the relationship between the Polarization vector \(\vec{P}\) and the electric field \(\vec{E}\): $$ \vec{P} = \epsilon_0 \chi_e \vec{E} $$

Question 43: Write Faraday's law of electromagnetic induction in differential form.

The differential form is: $$ \nabla \times \vec{E} = -\frac{\partial \vec{B}}{\partial t} $$

Question 44: The electric field is \( \vec{E} = 2x\hat{i} + 2y\hat{j} + 3\hat{k} \). Calculate volume charge density.

From Gauss's Law, \( \rho = \epsilon_0 (\nabla \cdot \vec{E}) \).

\( \nabla \cdot \vec{E} = \frac{\partial(2x)}{\partial x} + \frac{\partial(2y)}{\partial y} + \frac{\partial(3)}{\partial z} = 2 + 2 + 0 = 4 \).

So, the charge density is constant: \( \rho = 4\epsilon_0 \) C/m³.

Question 45: What is the magnetic field at a point 3 cm from a long straight wire with a 25 A current?

Using Ampere's Law, \( B = \frac{\mu_0 I}{2\pi r} = \frac{(4\pi \times 10^{-7})(25)}{2\pi(0.03)} \approx 1.67 \times 10^{-4} \) T or 0.167 mT.

Question 46: An inductor of 3H & resistance of 6\(\Omega\) is connected to a 12V battery. Calculate the initial rate of increase of current.

The circuit equation is \(V = IR + L\frac{dI}{dt}\). Initially, at \(t=0\), current \(I=0\).

So, \(12 = (0)(6) + 3\frac{dI}{dt}\), which gives \( \frac{dI}{dt} = \frac{12}{3} = 4 \) A/s.

Question 47: Define magnetic induction & intensity of magnetisation.

  • Magnetic Induction (\(\vec{B}\)): The total magnetic field at a point, including contributions from external sources and the material itself. Unit: Tesla (T).
  • Intensity of Magnetisation (\(\vec{M}\)): The net magnetic dipole moment developed per unit volume of a magnetic material. Unit: Amperes per meter (A/m).

Question 48: Define co-efficient of self-inductance & give its units.

Definition: A measure of a circuit's ability to oppose a change in current, defined as the ratio of magnetic flux linkage to the current (\(L = N\Phi/I\)) or as the constant in the back-EMF equation (\(\epsilon = -L dI/dt\)).

Unit: The Henry (H).

Question 49: What is hysteresis loss?

Hysteresis loss is the energy dissipated as heat in a ferromagnetic material during cycles of magnetization. It is caused by the material's magnetization (B) lagging behind the magnetizing field (H). The loss per cycle is equal to the area of the material's B-H hysteresis loop.

Question 50: A charge Q coulomb is placed at the centre of a cube. The electric flux through each side of the cube is ______.

According to Gauss's Law, the total electric flux (\(\Phi_{total}\)) is \( Q_{enclosed} / \epsilon_0 = Q / \epsilon_0 \).

Since the charge is centered, the flux is distributed equally through the 6 identical faces. Therefore, the flux through a single face (\(\Phi_{face}\)) is: $$ \Phi_{face} = \frac{\Phi_{total}}{6} = \frac{Q}{6\epsilon_0} $$
